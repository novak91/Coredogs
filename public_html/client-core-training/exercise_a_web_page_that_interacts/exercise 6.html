Circumference and area of a circle

Theory:

Circumference is the linear distance around the outside
of a closed curve or circular object. The circumference of a circle is the length
around it.

Area is a quantity that expresses the extent of a two-dimensional surface (such as
circle) or shape in the plane.

Formula (circumference): c = Π * d
Formula (area): A = Π * r2
where:

  • c = circumference
  • Π = pi
  • d = diameter
  • A = area
  • r = radius
